The Hellenic Data Management Symposium (HDMS) is the annual forum for the Greek database community to present and discuss recent advances for data-intensive applications. The 17th HDMS will be held between July 8-9, 2019 in Athens, Greece.

In addition to state-of-the-art research from Greek researchers inside and outside of Greece, for the first time this year we will open the meeting to the Greek startup community. We have invited successful startups from Greek founders as well as successful Greek VCs to participate in the conference with presentations and posters in a dedicated “Greek startups” session and there will also be an entrepreneurship panel. Our current list of startups and VCs includes: https://my-netdata.io, https://lenses.io, https://www.inaccel.com, https://www.arrikto.com, https://www.raw-labs.com, https://marathon.vc.

Furthermore, HDMS 2019 will feature two keynotes:

  • Machine learning for data management and data management for machine learning, Alkis Polyzotis from Google
  • Apache Flink and data Artisans: A 5+ year journey, Kostas Tzoumas from Data Artisans
as well as an AI/DB panel.

Machine learning for data management and data management for machine learning

Abstract

This talk makes the case for the mutually beneficial relationship between ML and DBs. The first part of the talk describes how ML enables us to rethink the design of the components in a database system. In particular, our work shows that we can recast data indexing as an ML problem and in doing so obtain smaller and faster indexes for a large class of use cases. The second part of the talk describes how we can adapt principles from data-management systems (such as declarative schema constraints and declarative queries) in order to improve the robustness and quality of ML models. This part is based on our ongoing work in TensorFlow Extended, Google's internal platform for production ML pipelines.

Bio

Alkis Polyzotis is a research scientist at Google Research, where he currently leads the data-management projects in Google’s TensorFlow Extended (TFX) platform. His interests include data management for machine learning, enterprise data search, and interactive data exploration. Before joining Google, he was a professor at UC Santa Cruz. He received a PhD in Computer Sciences from the University of Wisconsin at Madison and a diploma in engineering from the National Tech. University of Athens, Greece.

Apache Flink and data Artisans: A 5+ year journey

Abstract

n 2014, some PhD students and postdocs started an open source project called Apache Flink originating in their research at the Technical University of Berlin, as well as a VC-funded company called data Artisans and recently renamed to Ververica that recently joined forces with Alibaba Group to further accelerate the growth of the technology.

Flink is now the definite framework for real-time stream processing, one of the most widely deployed Big Data frameworks globally boasting users such as Uber, Netflix, Lyft, Airbnb, Yelp, Alibaba Group, Tencent and many others, and supported by a community of over 500 contributors.

In this talk I will go through some personal learnings on building an innovative technology, and open source community from the ground up, as well as a business model to support and monetize the technology.

Bio

Kostas Tzoumas co-founded and has been the CEO of data Artisans (recently renamed to Ververica) since 2014. data Artisans was founded by the original creators of Apache Flink to bring the technology to the market, and recently joined forces with Alibaba Group to further accelerate the technological innovation and community growth in Apache Flink. Before founding data Artisans Kostas received a PhD from Aalborg University and was a postdoctoral researcher at the Technical University of Berlin.

TOPICS



We invite submissions in the following three tracks:
(A) Research, (B) Working Ideas, and (C) Demos.

Topics of interest include but are not limited to:

Core database technologies (e.g., query processing and optimization, storage and indexing, database concurrency control, OLTP and OLAP processing).

Models and languages for the management of data (e.g., XML, RDF, probabilistic databases, meta-data management, multi-media, graph data management).

NoSQL and Cloud technologies (e.g., storage, scalability)

Techniques for high performance and scalability in the management of data (e.g., distributed data processing, hardware accelerators, new hardware technologies).

Emerging data management architectures (e.g., data stream management, peer-to-peer systems, mobile data management, blockchain).

Confluence of data management with: information retrieval, knowledge management, natural language processing, and data mining.

Data management applications (e.g., geographical information systems, data warehouses, social networks, scientific databases, IoT, crowd sourcing).

Other aspects of modern information systems (e.g., security, privacy, user interfaces, energy use minimization).


Τhe following evaluation principles will apply

RESEARCH TRACK
Submissions recently accepted at top international conferences or journals are automatically accepted. Examples include SIGMOD, VLDB, PODS, ICDE, EDBT, and SIGIR. Each submission should include the DOI link of the original paper.

WORKING IDEAS
The Working Ideas track accepts original work, ongoing work, and position/vision statements. Authors should submit a 1 page PDF in ACM SIGMOD format. Submissions will be reviewed by the PC and chosen for presentation or poster.

DEMOS
This track accepts demonstrations of innovative data-intensive applications, systems, algorithms, etc. Authors should submit a 1 page PDF in ACM SIGMOD format. Submissions will be reviewed by the PC. If submitting an already published demonstration at a top conference, only the DOI is needed (and it will be an automatic accept).

Conference Format:

The conference format will be optimized for interactivity. We will have few long talks and many shorter talks (10 minutes) and posters.
